Hacker Teams

Understanding Hacker Types

Hackers are categorized based on their intentions, methodologies, and the teams they work with.

Red Team

Offensive Security

The Red Team simulates real-world attacks to test an organization's security defenses. They use the same tools and techniques as malicious hackers to identify vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.

Blue Team

Defensive Security

The Blue Team focuses on defending systems against attacks. They monitor, detect, and respond to security incidents, implementing protective measures and developing security policies.

Purple Team

Collaborative Security

The Purple Team combines the offensive skills of the Red Team with the defensive capabilities of the Blue Team. They work together to maximize the effectiveness of security operations.

White Hat

Ethical Hackers

White Hat hackers use their skills for ethical purposes. They help organizations identify and fix security vulnerabilities before malicious hackers can exploit them.

Black Hat

Malicious Hackers

Black Hat hackers exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain, financial profit, or malicious purposes. Their activities are illegal and can cause significant damage to organizations and individuals.

Gray Hat

Between Ethical and Malicious

Gray Hat hackers operate in the middle ground between White and Black Hats. They may find vulnerabilities without permission but disclose them to the organization rather than exploiting them.

Attack Scenarios

Common Hacking Scenarios

Understanding the methods hackers use to compromise secure systems.

Phishing Attacks

Social Engineering

Hackers create fake emails, websites, or messages that appear legitimate to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

Common techniques:
  • Spoofed email addresses
  • Fake login pages
  • Malicious attachments
  • Urgent requests for information

Information Gathering

Reconnaissance

Before launching an attack, hackers collect information about their target. This includes scanning for open ports, identifying running services, and gathering information about employees.

Common techniques:
  • Port scanning
  • OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)
  • Social media profiling
  • DNS enumeration

Vulnerability Exploitation

Finding and Exploiting Weaknesses

Hackers identify and exploit vulnerabilities in software, operating systems, or web applications to gain unauthorized access or control.

Common techniques:
  • SQL injection
  • Cross-site scripting (XSS)
  • Buffer overflows
  • Privilege escalation

Social Engineering

Manipulating Human Psychology

Hackers exploit human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. They manipulate people into breaking security protocols or revealing confidential information.

Common techniques:
  • Pretexting (creating a fabricated scenario)
  • Baiting (offering something enticing)
  • Tailgating (following someone into a secure area)
  • Impersonation (posing as IT support or executives)

Wireless Network Attacks

Compromising Wi-Fi Security

Hackers target wireless networks to intercept data, gain unauthorized access, or perform man-in-the-middle attacks.

Common techniques:
  • Evil twin attacks (fake access points)
  • WPA/WPA2 password cracking
  • Packet sniffing
  • Deauthentication attacks

Malware Deployment

Infecting Systems

Hackers create and distribute malicious software designed to damage systems, steal data, or provide unauthorized access.

Common techniques:
  • Drive-by downloads
  • Malicious email attachments
  • Infected USB drives
  • Software backdoors
